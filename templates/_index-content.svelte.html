Election 2020

Minnesotans Googled these Democratic candidates the most throughout 2019

By Jeff Hargarten
Star Tribune January 13, 2020

Elections in the digital age can provide multiple ways of measuring the electorate's enthusiasm for a candidate beyond traditional measures like polling, fundraising and endorsements.

Search interest is one of those metrics that can reveal which candidates are drawing more attention from the general public. Data provided by Google Trends shows which Democratic primary candidates Minnesotans have been searching for most throughout 2019.

As more data is made available, this tracker will be updated.

How curiosity about candidates has changed

Three Democratic primary candidates have drawn the most search interest from Minnesotans in 2019 so far: former Vice President Joe Biden, Sen. Bernie Sanders (D,VT) and the state's own Sen. Amy Klobuchar.

Google Trends rates interest of a search term on a scale from 0 to 100. Those rated 100 – in this case Biden – have the maximum amount of search interest for a candidate, and other candidate's scores are presented as comparisons to that number.

Most searched candidates by county

Across 2019, Joe Biden was the most searched candidate in many Minnesota counties, while curiosity about Amy Klobuchar and Bernie Sanders also covered significant ground.

Elizabeth Warren, Pete Buttigieg, Tulsi Gabbard and Andrew Yang also took some scattered top spots.
